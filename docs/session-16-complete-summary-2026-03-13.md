### History Unit 2 (Started)

#### 1. Jazz Age and Great Depression (h2c1)

**Roaring Twenties (1920-1929)**:
- Economic boom: GDP grew 42%, industrial production doubled
- New industries: Automobiles (15 million Model T sold), radio, movies, electricity
- Social changes: Women's suffrage (1920), flappers, Prohibition, Jazz Age
- Republican policies: Laissez-faire, tax cuts, tariffs, immigration restriction
- Inequality: Top 1% owned 40% of wealth, agriculture depression

**Stock Market Crash (October 1929)**:
- Speculation and buying on margin (10% down payment)
- Black Thursday (October 24): 12.9 million shares traded
- Black Tuesday (October 29): 16.4 million shares, Dow fell 12%
- Aftermath: Dow fell 89% (381 to 41), $30 billion lost

**Great Depression Causes**:
- Stock market crash, bank failures (9,000 banks)
- Overproduction and underconsumption
- International factors (Smoot-Hawley Tariff)
- Federal Reserve mistakes
- Dust Bowl (1930-1936)

**Great Depression Impact**:
- Unemployment: 3% (1929) → 25% (1933), 13 million unemployed
- GDP collapsed 30%, industrial production fell 50%
- Social impact: Hoovervilles, breadlines, migration, family breakdown

**Hoover's Response (1929-1933)**:
- Philosophy: Rugged individualism, opposed direct relief
- RFC (1932): Lent to banks, not individuals
- Smoot-Hawley Tariff (1930): Worsened depression
- Bonus Army (1932): Public relations disaster
- Lost 1932 election in landslide

**Franklin D. Roosevelt and New Deal**:
- First 100 Days (March-June 1933): 15 major laws
- Bank Holiday: Restored confidence
- Fireside Chats: 30 radio addresses

**New Deal Programs**:
- Relief: FERA, CCC (3 million employed), WPA (8.5 million employed)
- Recovery: NIRA, AAA, TVA, NRA
- Reform: Glass-Steagall (FDIC), SEC, Social Security (1935), Wagner Act, Fair Labor Standards Act

**New Deal Opposition**:
- Supreme Court: Struck down NIRA, AAA
- Business: Accused FDR of socialism
- Left-wing: Huey Long, Father Coughlin
- Conservative: Deficit spending criticism

**New Deal Evaluation**:
- Successes: Restored confidence, unemployment fell to 14% (1937), banking stabilized
- Failures: Didn't end depression (WWII did), unemployment still 17% (1939)
- Roosevelt Recession (1937-1938): Proved Keynesian argument
- Long-term: Expanded federal government, created welfare state

#### 2. WWII and Post-War America (h2c2)

**America's Path to War**:
- Isolationism: Neutrality Acts (1935-1937), 95% opposed war
- From neutrality to intervention: Cash and Carry (1939), Destroyers-for-Bases (1940), Lend-Lease (1941)
- Atlantic Charter (August 1941): FDR and Churchill war aims

**Pearl Harbor (December 7, 1941)**:
- Background: US-Japan tensions, oil embargo (July 1941)
- Attack: 353 Japanese aircraft, 2,403 Americans killed, 8 battleships damaged
- FDR speech: 'A date which will live in infamy'
- Germany and Italy declared war (December 11)

**Home Front - Economic Mobilization**:
- War Production Board: Converted civilian industries
- Production miracle: 300,000 aircraft, 88,000 tanks, 3,000 ships
- Full employment: Unemployment 1% (1944)
- Government spending: $9 billion (1940) → $95 billion (1945)
- GDP doubled: $100 billion → $200 billion

**Home Front - Women and Minorities**:
- Women: 6 million entered workforce, 'Rosie the Riveter'
- African Americans: 1 million served (segregated), Double V Campaign
- Japanese Americans: 120,000 interned (1942-1945), Korematsu v. US
- Mexican Americans: Bracero Program, Zoot Suit Riots (1943)

**Military Strategy**:
- Europe First: North Africa, Italy, D-Day (June 6, 1944), Germany surrendered (May 8, 1945)
- Pacific: Island hopping, Midway, Guadalcanal, Iwo Jima, Okinawa
- Firebombing: Tokyo (March 1945) - 100,000 killed

**Atomic Bomb**:
- Manhattan Project: $2 billion, 130,000 workers
- Hiroshima (August 6, 1945): 140,000 dead by year end
- Nagasaki (August 9, 1945): 70,000 dead by year end
- Japan surrendered (August 15, 1945)
- Debate: Saved lives vs war crime, atomic diplomacy

**Post-War Economic Boom (1945-1960)**:
- Pent-up demand, GI Bill (8 million attended college)
- Suburbanization: Levittown, 13 million homes built
- Baby boom: 76 million babies (1946-1964)
- Consumerism: TV (50 million sets by 1960), cars (60 million by 1960)
- GDP: $200 billion (1945) → $500 billion (1960)
- Median income rose 30%, home ownership 44% → 62%

**Post-War Society**:
- Conformity: Suburban life, organization man, gender roles
- Anxiety: Nuclear fear, Cold War paranoia
- Youth culture: Rock and roll, Elvis Presley
- Television: 90% of homes by 1960

**Cold War Context**:
- Truman Doctrine (1947): Support free peoples
- Marshall Plan (1947): $13 billion to Europe
- NATO (1949): Military alliance
- Korean War (1950-1953): 36,000 Americans killed

**McCarthyism**:
- Origins: USSR atomic bomb (1949), China fell (1949), Alger Hiss case
- Joseph McCarthy: Senator from Wisconsin, Wheeling speech (1950)
- Tactics: Accusations without evidence, guilt by association
- Targets: State Department, Army, Hollywood (blacklist)
- Downfall: Army-McCarthy hearings (1954), Senate censure
- Impact: Careers destroyed, self-censorship, weakened left

**Eisenhower Presidency (1953-1961)**:
- Modern Republicanism: Accepted New Deal, Interstate Highway System
- Civil rights: Little Rock (1957)
- Foreign policy: Massive retaliation, CIA covert operations
- Farewell address: Warned of military-industrial complex
