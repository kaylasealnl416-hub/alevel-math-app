### 2. Colonial India (h4c5)

**East India Company (1600-1857)**:
- Trading posts to territorial control
- Battle of Plassey (1757), Diwani rights (1765)
- Economic exploitation: Deindustrialization, drain of wealth
- India's GDP share fell from 23% (1700) to 4% (1950)

**1857 Rebellion**:
- Causes: Political, economic, social, religious, military
- Trigger: Enfield rifle cartridges
- Events: Meerut to Delhi, spread across north India
- Suppression: Brutal, both sides committed atrocities
- Consequences: End of Company rule, Crown took control

**British Raj (1858-1947)**:
- Administration: Viceroy, Indian Civil Service, princely states
- Economic: Railways, irrigation, but continued exploitation
- Social: English education, Western ideas, caste codification
- Racism: Segregation, 'white man's burden'

**Indian National Congress (1885)**:
- Moderate demands initially
- Partition of Bengal (1905), Swadeshi movement
- Extremists: Tilak, Lajpat Rai, Pal

**Muslim League (1906)**:
- Separate electorates (1909)
- Muhammad Ali Jinnah
- Lucknow Pact (1916)

**Mohandas Gandhi (1869-1948)**:
- Satyagraha (non-violent resistance)
- Non-Cooperation Movement (1920-1922)
- Salt March (1930) - 240 miles, international attention
- Quit India Movement (1942)
- Assassination (1948)

**World War II**:
- 2.5 million Indians served
- Congress opposed, Muslim League supported
- Bengal famine (1943) - 2-3 million died
- Cripps Mission (1942), Cabinet Mission (1946) failed

**Partition and Independence (1947)**:
- Mountbatten Plan
- August 15, 1947 - independence
- 10-20 million refugees, 200,000-2 million killed
- Kashmir conflict ongoing

**Post-Independence**:
- India: Democracy, secularism, Nehru era
- Pakistan: Instability, military coups, Bangladesh (1971)
- Nuclear rivals

**Historiographical Debates**:
- Colonial impact: Development vs underdevelopment
- 1857: Mutiny vs First War of Independence
- Gandhi: Effectiveness, communalism, caste, women
- Partition: Inevitability, responsibility, violence
- British motives for leaving

### 3. Collapse of Soviet Union (h4c6)

**Pre-Gorbachev Stagnation**:
- Brezhnev era (1964-1982): Economic stagnation, military spending
- Afghanistan War (1979-1989): Soviet Vietnam
- Chernobyl (1986): Exposed incompetence
- Economic and political problems

**Mikhail Gorbachev (1985-1991)**:
- Youngest leader since Stalin
- Reformer, but unintended consequences

**Perestroika (Restructuring)**:
- Economic reform: Decentralization, private enterprise
- Law on State Enterprises (1987), cooperatives (1988)
- Failure: Economy worsened, GDP fell

**Glasnost (Openness)**:
- Freedom of speech, media freedom
- Released dissidents, revealed Stalin's crimes
- Undermined Communist Party legitimacy

**Democratization**:
- Congress of People's Deputies (1989)
- Boris Yeltsin emerged as rival
- Article 6 repealed (1990) - ended Communist monopoly

**New Thinking in Foreign Policy**:
- End Cold War, reduce military spending
- Sinatra Doctrine: Eastern Europe free to choose
- INF Treaty (1987), START Treaty (1991)
- Afghanistan withdrawal (1989)

**Eastern European Revolutions (1989)**:
- Poland: Solidarity won elections
- Hungary: Opened border with Austria
- East Germany: Berlin Wall fell (November 9)
- Czechoslovakia: Velvet Revolution
- Romania: Violent revolution, Ceaușescu executed

**Fall of Berlin Wall (November 9, 1989)**:
- Mass protests, government announcement
- Crowds tore down wall
- Symbol of Cold War's end
- German reunification (October 3, 1990)

**Baltic Independence**:
- Singing Revolution (1987-1991)
- Baltic Way (August 23, 1989) - 2 million human chain
- Independence declarations (1990)
- Recognition (September 1991)

**August Coup (August 19-21, 1991)**:
- Hardliners detained Gorbachev
- Yeltsin defied coup, stood on tank
- Coup collapsed, accelerated Soviet collapse

**Dissolution (1991)**:
- Ukraine independence (August 24)
- Belavezha Accords (December 8) - USSR dissolved
- Gorbachev resignation (December 25)
- 15 new countries, end of Cold War

**Causes of Collapse**:
- Economic: Inefficiency, military spending, oil prices, technology gap
- Political: Legitimacy crisis, glasnost, democratization, nationalism
- International: Afghanistan, Eastern Europe, arms race

**Post-Soviet Era**:
- Yeltsin (1991-1999): Shock therapy, oligarchs, Chechen wars
- Putin (2000-present): Authoritarianism, assertive foreign policy
- Baltic states: EU and NATO (2004)
- Ukraine: Orange Revolution (2004), Euromaidan (2013-2014), Russian aggression (2014, 2022)
- Central Asia: Authoritarian regimes
- Caucasus: Conflicts (Georgia 2008, Nagorno-Karabakh)

**End of Cold War**:
- US sole superpower, 'unipolar moment'
- NATO expansion
- Globalization, optimism ('end of history')
- New conflicts (Yugoslavia, terrorism)
- Russia resentment

**Historiographical Debates**:
- Why collapse: Economic determinism vs Gorbachev's role vs Reagan vs nationalism
- Gorbachev: Hero or villain
- Yeltsin: Democrat or opportunist
- End of Cold War: Who won, peaceful end, NATO expansion
- Post-Soviet transition: Shock therapy, democracy, oligarchs, Putin
