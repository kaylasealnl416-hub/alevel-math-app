## 🎯 Content Additions

### Advanced Research Methods (psy3c4)

**Experimental Designs**:
- Repeated measures design (within-subjects, advantages: controls participant variables, fewer participants needed; disadvantages: order effects, demand characteristics; control: counterbalancing)
- Independent groups design (between-subjects, advantages: no order effects; disadvantages: participant variables; control: random allocation)
- Matched pairs design (advantages: controls participant variables, no order effects; disadvantages: time-consuming, cannot match all variables)

**Confounding Variables and Controls**:
- Types of confounding variables (participant variables, situational variables, demand characteristics, experimenter bias)
- Control methods (standardization, randomization, single blind, double blind)

**Descriptive Statistics**:
- Measures of central tendency (mean, median, mode - advantages and disadvantages)
- Measures of dispersion (range, standard deviation - interpretation and use)

**Inferential Statistics**:
- Hypothesis testing (null hypothesis, alternative hypothesis, significance level p < 0.05)
- Type I error (false positive - reject null when true, 5% chance)
- Type II error (false negative - retain null when false)

**Statistical Tests**:
- **T-tests**: Independent t-test (compare two independent groups), Paired t-test (compare two related groups)
- **ANOVA**: One-way ANOVA (compare 3+ groups, avoids multiple comparisons problem), Post-hoc tests (identify which groups differ)
- **Correlation**: Correlation coefficient r (-1 to +1, positive/negative/no correlation, strength: weak/moderate/strong), Limitation: correlation ≠ causation
- **Chi-square**: Test relationship between categorical variables, contingency table, χ² value and p-value

**Qualitative Methods**:
- **Interviews**: Structured (fixed questions, standardized), Semi-structured (fixed + probes, flexible), Unstructured (conversational, rich data)
- **Observations**: Naturalistic (natural environment, high ecological validity), Controlled (lab, control variables), Participant observation (insider perspective)
- **Thematic analysis**: 6 steps (familiarization, coding, search themes, review themes, define themes, write up)
- **Grounded theory**: Theory emerges from data (not testing pre-existing theory)

**Reliability**:
- Internal reliability (consistency within measure, split-half reliability, Cronbach's alpha > 0.7)
- External reliability (test-retest reliability, consistency over time)
- Inter-rater reliability (consistency between observers, agreement calculation)

**Validity**:
- Internal validity (does study measure what it claims? control confounds)
- External validity:
  - Population validity (generalize to other people? representative sample)
  - Ecological validity (generalize to real-world? naturalistic settings)
  - Temporal validity (generalize to other time periods? historical changes)

**Sampling Techniques**:
- Random sampling (equal chance, unbiased, representative)
- Opportunity sampling (available people, quick/easy, biased)
- Volunteer sampling (advertise, willing participants, biased - volunteers differ)
- Stratified sampling (sample from subgroups, representative of strata)

**Pilot Studies**:
- Purpose (test procedures, identify problems, check materials, estimate time, train researchers)
- Benefits (save time/money, improve validity, improve reliability)

**Ethical Considerations**:
- BPS Code of Ethics:
  - Informed consent (know what study involves, voluntary agreement)
  - Deception (avoid misleading, debrief if necessary)
  - Protection from harm (minimize physical/psychological harm)
  - Right to withdraw (can leave anytime, withdraw data)
- Additional principles:
  - Confidentiality (personal information private, data anonymized)
  - Privacy (don't observe in private settings without consent)
  - Debriefing (explain true purpose, check for harm)
  - Special populations (extra protection for vulnerable groups - children, prisoners)
  - Cost-benefit analysis (weigh benefits vs. costs)

**Evaluation of Research Methods**:
- Quantitative strengths (objective, replicable, statistical analysis, large samples)
- Quantitative limitations (artificial, superficial, demand characteristics)
- Qualitative strengths (rich data, ecological validity, flexible, participant perspective)
- Qualitative limitations (subjective, not replicable, small samples, time-consuming)
- Mixed methods (triangulation, complementarity)
