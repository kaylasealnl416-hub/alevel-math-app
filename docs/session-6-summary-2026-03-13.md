## 🎯 Revenue, Costs & Profit (e3c2) - Comprehensive Enhancement

### Content Growth
- **Before**: 15 key points
- **After**: 43 key points
- **Growth**: +186.7% (28 new points)

### Major Additions

#### 1. Revenue Concepts Expanded
- ✅ Total Revenue (TR): Linear in perfect competition, curved in imperfect competition
- ✅ Average Revenue (AR): AR = Price, AR curve is demand curve
- ✅ Marginal Revenue (MR): Perfect competition (MR = AR = P), Imperfect competition (MR < AR)
- ✅ Revenue relationships: MR curve has twice the slope of AR curve

#### 2. Cost Concepts Detailed
- ✅ Fixed Costs (FC): Rent, salaries, insurance, loan interest, depreciation
- ✅ Variable Costs (VC): Raw materials, wages, electricity, packaging
- ✅ Total Costs (TC): FC + VC, starts at FC
- ✅ Average Fixed Cost (AFC): FC / Q, continuously falling (rectangular hyperbola)
- ✅ Average Variable Cost (AVC): VC / Q, U-shaped, minimum is shutdown point
- ✅ Average Total Cost (AC): TC / Q = AFC + AVC, U-shaped
- ✅ Marginal Cost (MC): ΔTC / ΔQ, U-shaped, intersects AVC and AC at minimum points

#### 3. Graphical Analysis - Cost Curves Relationships
- ✅ MC intersects AVC at minimum AVC
- ✅ MC intersects AC at minimum AC
- ✅ Key insight: If marginal < average, average falls; if marginal > average, average rises
- ✅ Example: Exam score analogy (marginal score affects average)

#### 4. Short Run vs Long Run Costs
- ✅ Short run: At least one factor fixed (capital), FC exists, move along SRAC
- ✅ Long run: All factors variable, no FC, choose optimal factory size
- ✅ LRAC curve: Envelope of SRAC curves, U-shaped (economies → constant returns → diseconomies)
- ✅ LRAC sections: Economies of scale (falling), constant returns (flat), diseconomies (rising)
- ✅ Minimum LRAC: Minimum Efficient Scale (MES)

#### 5. Profit Concepts
- ✅ Normal profit: Opportunity cost of capital and entrepreneur's time (£50,000 example)
- ✅ Supernormal profit: π = TR - TC > 0, attracts entry, funds R&D
- ✅ Loss: π < 0, continue if TR > TVC (short run), exit if persistent (long run)

#### 6. Profit Maximization Rule - MR = MC
- ✅ Logic: If MR > MC → increase output, if MR < MC → decrease output
- ✅ Applies to all market structures
- ✅ Graphical analysis: Find MR = MC, read Q*, P* from demand, AC* from AC curve
- ✅ Profit = (P* - AC*) × Q* (shaded rectangle)

#### 7. Shutdown and Exit Decisions
- ✅ Shutdown point (short run): P = AVC (minimum average variable cost)
- ✅ Logic: If P > AVC, cover VC and contribute to FC → continue
- ✅ If P < AVC, don't cover VC → shut down (loss = FC)
- ✅ Exit point (long run): P = AC (if P < AC, make loss → exit)

#### 8. Real-World Examples (4 cases)

**Example 1 - Amazon Cost Structure**:
- FC: £20bn/year (warehouses, technology, salaries)
- VC: £200bn/year (products, shipping, packaging)
- Revenue: £400bn/year (2022)
- Insight: Retail low margins (high VC), AWS high margins (low VC, high FC)
- Amazon uses AWS profit to subsidize retail expansion

**Example 2 - UK Airline Profit Maximization**:
- British Airways (2019): MR = MC at 45m passengers/year
- Price: £150/ticket, AC: £120/ticket, Profit: £30/ticket
- Total profit: £1.35bn
- COVID-19 (2020): MR < MC at all output → shut down most flights

**Example 3 - Restaurant Shutdown Decision**:
- Revenue: £5,000/week, VC: £4,000/week, FC: £2,000/week
- Loss: -£1,000/week
- Decision: Continue (TR > TVC, contributes £1,000 to FC)
- If shut down: Loss = £2,000/week (still pay rent)
- Long run: Exit if losses persist

**Example 4 - Tesla Economies of Scale**:
- 2010: 1,000 cars, AC = £80,000/car
- 2015: 50,000 cars, AC = £50,000/car
- 2020: 500,000 cars, AC = £35,000/car
- 2023: 1.8m cars, AC = £30,000/car
- LRAC falling (economies of scale), MES ~2m cars/year
- Beyond 5m cars/year: May face diseconomies of scale
