- ✅ **Japan's Lost Decades (1991-2010)**: 20 years of stagnation after rapid growth. Causes: asset bubble burst, banking crisis, deflation, aging population, zombie companies. Lesson: Demographic decline and deflation are hard to overcome

- ✅ **Botswana's Success (1966-2020)**: Africa's fastest-growing economy. Key factors: diamond wealth managed prudently, good governance, low corruption, investment in education. GDP per capita: $400 → $7,600

## 🎯 Exchange Rates (e4c4) - Comprehensive Enhancement

### Content Growth
- **Before**: 9 key points
- **After**: 41 key points
- **Growth**: +356% (32 new points)

### Major Additions

#### 1. Graphical Analysis
- ✅ **Foreign exchange market diagram**: Supply and demand for currency, equilibrium determination
- ✅ **Appreciation**: Demand curve shifts right → exchange rate rises
- ✅ **Depreciation**: Supply curve shifts right → exchange rate falls

#### 2. Exchange Rate Systems - Detailed Comparison

**Floating Exchange Rate**:
- ✅ Definition and examples (USD, GBP, JPY, EUR)
- ✅ 4 advantages: automatic adjustment, monetary policy independence, no reserves needed, no speculative attacks
- ✅ 4 disadvantages: uncertainty, inflation risk, speculation, may not self-correct

**Fixed Exchange Rate**:
- ✅ Definition and examples (Hong Kong dollar, Saudi riyal, Danish krone)
- ✅ How it works: central bank intervention mechanism
- ✅ 3 advantages: certainty, discipline, inflation anchor
- ✅ 4 disadvantages: loss of monetary policy independence, requires reserves, vulnerable to attacks, no automatic adjustment

**Managed Float (Dirty Float)**:
- ✅ Definition and examples (China, India, Singapore)
- ✅ 4 intervention methods: direct intervention, interest rates, capital controls, verbal intervention

#### 3. Factors Affecting Exchange Rates (7 detailed factors)
- ✅ Interest rates (with Bank of England example)
- ✅ Inflation rates (PPP theory)
- ✅ Current account balance (Germany surplus example)
- ✅ Economic performance (US tech boom 1990s)
- ✅ Speculation (George Soros 1992)
- ✅ Government intervention
- ✅ Political stability (Brexit 2016 - pound fell 10%)

#### 4. Effects of Appreciation/Depreciation
- ✅ **On trade**: SPICED mnemonic, Marshall-Lerner condition
- ✅ **On inflation**: Import price effects, strong dollar example (2014-2016)
- ✅ **On growth**: AD effects, expenditure-switching
- ✅ **On employment**: Sectoral impacts, real wage effects

#### 5. J-Curve Effect
- ✅ Definition: short-run worsening before long-run improvement
- ✅ Marshall-Lerner condition: elasticities must sum to > 1
- ✅ Time lag: typically 6-18 months
- ✅ Oil-importing countries example (inelastic demand)

#### 6. Real-World Case Studies (4 major currency crises)
- ✅ **1997 Asian Financial Crisis**: Thai baht collapsed 50%, contagion spread to Indonesia (rupiah fell 80%), South Korea, Malaysia, Philippines. Capital flight $100bn, IMF bailouts $110bn. Causes: overvalued pegs, current account deficits, short-term debt, weak banks. Impact: GDP fell 10-15%, unemployment tripled. Lesson: Fixed pegs vulnerable to speculative attacks
- ✅ **2015 Swiss Franc Shock**: SNB abandoned Euro peg without warning, franc appreciated 30% in minutes. Background: SNB had pegged at 1.20 per Euro since 2011, reserves grew to 500bn francs. Impact: exporters devastated, forex borrowers faced huge losses, some brokers bankrupted. Lesson: Even well-resourced central banks cannot maintain unsustainable pegs
- ✅ **Plaza Accord (1985)**: G5 countries agreed to depreciate US dollar. Dollar fell 50% against yen and mark. Impact on Japan: yen appreciation → BoJ cut rates → asset bubble → bubble burst (1991) → lost decades. Lesson: Exchange rate manipulation can have unintended long-term consequences
- ✅ **Black Wednesday (1992)**: UK forced out of ERM. George Soros bet against pound, Bank of England spent £27bn reserves, raised rates to 15%, but failed. Pound fell 15%. Impact: short-term pain but long-term gain - UK recovered faster than Eurozone. Lesson: Markets can overwhelm central banks if peg unsustainable
