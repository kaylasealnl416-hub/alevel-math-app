#### 2.3 Market Failure (e1c5)

**Added**:
- ✅ Detailed negative externality graph analysis (MPC, MSC, MEC curves)
- ✅ Detailed positive externality graph analysis (MPB, MSB, MEB curves)
- ✅ Deadweight loss explanation with graphical representation
- ✅ Real-world examples:
  - Negative: Carbon emissions from coal power plants
  - Positive: COVID-19 vaccination (herd immunity)
- ✅ **6 Policy Solutions with detailed analysis**:

**Policy Tool 1 - Pigouvian Tax**:
- Mechanism: Tax = marginal external cost
- Case study: Sweden Carbon Tax (1991-present, $27 → $137/ton)
- Results: GDP +78%, emissions -29% (1990-2020), biofuel use 0% → 30%
- Advantages: Government revenue, market mechanism, innovation incentive
- Disadvantages: Difficult to measure, regressive, carbon leakage, unpopular

**Policy Tool 2 - Subsidies**:
- Mechanism: Payment = marginal external benefit
- Case study: UK University Tuition Subsidies (£7,000/student/year)
- Advantages: Increases merit goods, politically popular, targeted
- Disadvantages: Opportunity cost, overconsumption risk, hard to remove

**Policy Tool 3 - Regulation**:
- Examples: Emission limits, safety standards, mandatory education
- Advantages: Certainty, simple, can ban harmful activities
- Disadvantages: Inflexible, no incentive to exceed, high monitoring costs

**Policy Tool 4 - Tradable Pollution Permits**:
- Case study: EU Emissions Trading System (40% of EU emissions, €80/ton)
- Advantages: Certainty of total pollution, cost-effective, innovation incentive
- Disadvantages: Allocation controversy, price volatility, monitoring costs

**Policy Tool 5 - Property Rights (Coase Theorem)**:
- Case study: New Zealand fishing quotas (1986)
- Advantages: Market-based, no government intervention, efficient
- Disadvantages: High transaction costs, difficult for diffuse externalities

**Policy Tool 6 - Information Provision**:
- Examples: Health warnings, energy labels, nutrition information
- Advantages: Low cost, preserves choice, addresses information failure
- Disadvantages: Assumes rationality, slow behavior change, ineffective for addiction

### 3. Real-World Case Studies ✅

#### 3.1 National Income (e2c4) - 2008 Financial Crisis

**Added**:
- ✅ Complete case study of multiplier effect in 2008 crisis
- ✅ Initial shock: Investment fell $1 trillion (2007-2009)
- ✅ Negative multiplier process: Investment ↓ → Jobs ↓ → Consumption ↓ → More jobs ↓
- ✅ Multiplier calculation: MPC = 0.8, k = 5, theoretical GDP decline = $5 trillion
- ✅ Actual outcome: GDP -2.5% (2009), unemployment 10%
- ✅ Government response: $787 billion stimulus (ARRA 2009)
- ✅ Recovery: Positive growth in 2010, but 6 years to restore employment
- ✅ Lessons learned: (1) Multiplier works both ways, (2) Financial shocks amplify, (3) Government intervention stabilizes, (4) Recovery takes time

**Content Growth**: 14 key points → 28 key points (100% increase)

**Added**:
- ✅ Complete case study of China joining WTO (December 11, 2001)

- ✅ Commitments: Tariff reduction (15.3% → 9.8%), open services, IP protection
- ✅ Trade impact: Exports $266B → $3.36T (2001-2022), 4% → 15% global share
